## Preamble

The present Cookie Policy explicates the manner in which **CodeFryDev** employs cookies, local storage, session storage, pixels, and analogous persistence technologies across the [CodeFryDev Ecosystem](https://codefrydev.in/). It supplements the [General Privacy Policy](/privacy/general/) and the [General Terms](/term/general/).

Should the User withhold assent, **the User must forthwith close the site** and configure browser settings to reject non-essential cookies.

---

## Definition of Cookies and Analogous Technologies

Cookies constitute small text files persisted upon the User's device. **Local storage** and **session storage** fulfil comparable persistence functions within modern browser architectures.

---

## Categories Employed

| Category | Purpose | Examples | Disabling Feasibility |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Strictly necessary** | Core functionality, security, session management | Theme preference, authentication tokens | Partially — may impair functionality |
| **Analytics** | Usage measurement, product improvement | Google Analytics (`G-VM01Q3R43D`) | Yes — browser settings, Google opt-out |
| **Performance / UX** | Session replay, heatmap analysis | Microsoft Clarity (`mxr4hxioh4`) | Yes — browser settings |
| **Functional** | Retention of user preferences | Tool settings, interface state | Often permissible |

The Service Provider **does not** presently employ cookies for third-party advertising retargeting unless expressly disclosed in an updated policy instrument.

---

## Managing Cookie Preferences

- **Browser configuration:** block or delete cookies (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge each furnish granular controls);
- **Google Analytics opt-out:** [Google tools](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout);
- **Do Not Track / Global Privacy Control:** honoured where technically feasible and legally mandated;
- **Cessation of use:** the most comprehensive opt-out from hosted analytics instrumentation.

---

## Retention Periods

Analytics cookie lifetimes conform to vendor defaults (Google, Microsoft) or shorter internal configurations where operationally available.
